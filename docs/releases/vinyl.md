# Vinyl

## Overview

Vinyl production requires specialized knowledge of manufacturing, mastering for analog format, and artwork specifications.

## Vinyl Format Specifications

### Technical Requirements
- Mastering: Specialized vinyl master
- Max continuous groove length: 30 minutes per side
- Groove diameter limitations
- Lacquer cutting specifications
- Pressing: 180g or 200g vinyl recommended

### Audio Considerations
- Dynamic range optimization
- Bass frequency management
- Inner groove distortion prevention
- Stereo separation for vinyl
- Reference monitoring

## Vinyl Mastering

### Pre-Mastering Checklist
- Original stereo mixes
- Specification review
- Timeline verification
- Vendor selection

### Mastering for Vinyl
- EQ adjustments for vinyl playback
- Dynamic range optimization
- Bass management
- Stereo phase considerations
- Test pressing ordering

## Vinyl Manufacturing

### Pressing Plants
- Allied Record Manufacturing
- Third Man Pressing
- Furnace Record Pressing
- RTI (Recording The Masters Inc.)

### Order Process
1. Lacquer creation
2. Test pressing
3. Quality verification
4. Approval and production
5. Pressing and packaging
6. Delivery

### Timeline
- Mastering: 1-2 weeks
- Lacquer cutting: 1 week
- Test pressing: 1 week
- Approval and production: 4-8 weeks
- Total: 7-12 weeks

## Vinyl Packaging

### Artwork Files
- Cover artwork (12" x 12")
- Back cover artwork
- Spine text
- Interior artwork
- Label artwork

### Materials
- Quality paper stock
- Die-cutting requirements
- Printing specifications
- Packaging options

## Vinyl Production Cost

- Mastering: $500-$1,500
- Test pressing: $100-$200 per copy
- Pressing: $1.50-$3.00 per unit (1,000+ quantity)
- Packaging: $0.50-$1.50 per unit
- Total per 1,000 units: $3,500-$7,500
