# Publishing 101

## Overview

Music publishing is the ownership and administration of musical compositions (songs) as distinct from sound recordings (masters).

## Publishing Fundamentals

### Composition vs. Recording
- **Composition**: The song (lyrics and melody)
- **Recording**: The specific performance and production

### Rights Holders
- **Songwriter**: Creator of composition
- **Publisher**: Administrator of composition rights
- **PRO**: Collects performance royalties

## Key Publishing Rights

### Mechanical Rights
- Right to reproduce composition
- Harry Fox Agency or MLC collection
- Per-unit royalty rate
- Statutory rate: $0.114 per track (as of 2024)

### Performance Rights
- Public performance of composition
- Radio, streaming, live venues
- Collected by PRO (ASCAP, BMI, SESAC)
- Songwriter and publisher split

### Synchronization Rights
- Use of composition with visual media
- Film, TV, advertising, games
- Direct licensing negotiation
- Custom royalty rates

### Print Rights
- Sheet music and lyrics
- Mechanical reproduction
- Educational use
- Direct licensing

## Publishing Ownership

### Full Ownership
- Songwriter retains all rights
- Self-publishes compositions
- Collects all royalties
- More complex administration

### Publisher Agreement
- Songwriter assigns some/all rights
- Publisher administers and collects
- Percentage split negotiated
- Term specified (duration)

### Co-Publishing
- Shared ownership and administration
- Multiple publishers possible
- Split royalty collection
- Coordination required

## PRO Overview

### Functions
- License public performances
- Collect performance royalties
- Distribute to members
- Track radio and streaming

### Major PROs
- ASCAP: American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers
- BMI: Broadcast Music, Inc.
- SESAC: Society of European Stage Authors and Composers

### Membership
- Free to join most PROs
- Registration of compositions
- Royalty statement access
- Payment distribution
