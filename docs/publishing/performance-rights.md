# Performance Rights

## Overview

Performance rights are the entitlement to collect royalties when compositions are publicly performed, primarily through broadcast and streaming.

## Performance Rights Overview

### What Triggers Performance Royalties
- Radio airplay
- Streaming playback
- Live venue performance
- Background music services
- Broadcast television
- Public events

### Who Collects
- ASCAP, BMI, SESAC (PROs in U.S.)
- International collection societies
- Streaming platforms (partial)
- Venue operators

## Performance Right Organization (PRO)

### ASCAP (American Society of Composers, Authors, Publishers)
- Membership: ~945,000
- Licensing: Radio, streaming, venues
- Distribution: Quarterly/semi-annual
- Emphasis: Composer advocacy

### BMI (Broadcast Music, Inc.)
- Membership: ~1 million+
- Licensing: Comprehensive
- Distribution: Quarterly/semi-annual
- Emphasis: Songwriter protection

### SESAC
- Membership: ~1 million+
- Licensing: Digital, broadcast
- Distribution: Monthly available
- Emphasis: Technology integration

## Registration Process

### Composition Registration
1. PRO membership
2. Composition details entry
3. Songwriter information
4. Publisher information
5. Confirmation and tracking

### Song Information
- Title (with alternate versions)
- Length/duration
- Songwriter name and split
- Publisher name and split
- Confirmation number

## Performance Tracking

### Radio Airplay
- Monitored by Nielsen SoundScan
- Billboard chart eligibility
- Royalty calculation basis
- Regular reporting

### Streaming Platforms
- Direct logging by platforms
- Automated reporting to PROs
- Real-time or batch processing
- Allocation to composers

## Royalty Distribution

### Payment Structure
- 50% to songwriter
- 50% to publisher
- Direct payment or through publisher
- Quarterly/semi-annual schedule

### Statement Analysis
- Quarterly/annual statements
- Track-by-track breakdown
- Territory attribution
- Discrepancy investigation
