# Royalties

## Overview

Royalties are payments earned from various uses of compositions and recordings, distributed through multiple channels.

## Types of Royalties

### Mechanical Royalties
- Per-unit payment for each reproduction
- Physical sales: CD, vinyl, cassette
- Digital downloads: iTunes, Bandcamp
- Streaming: All platforms
- Current rate: $0.114 per track
- Collection: Harry Fox, MLC, or direct

### Performance Royalties
- Public performance of composition
- Radio airplay
- Streaming platforms
- Live venues (ASCAP, BMI, SESAC)
- TV broadcast
- Split: 50% songwriter, 50% publisher

### Synchronization Royalties
- Visual media use (film, TV, ads, games)
- Custom negotiated rates
- Upfront license fees
- Backend participation

### Print Royalties
- Sheet music sales
- Educational materials
- Lyrics reproduction
- Digital sheet music (Musicnotes, etc.)

## Royalty Calculations

### Streaming Royalties
- Per-stream rate varies by platform
- Spotify: $0.003-$0.005 per stream
- Apple Music: $0.007 per stream
- YouTube: $0.002-$0.006 per stream
- Mechanical + performance components

### Album Sales Royalties
- Percentage of wholesale or retail
- Artist royalty: 10-20%
- Label retention: Remaining percentage
- Distribution costs deducted

### Advance Recoupment
- Advance deducted from royalties
- Royalties must exceed advance
- Label recoups before artist payment
- Recoupment status tracked

## Royalty Payment Processing

### Collection Paths
1. **Direct**: Platform pays label
2. **Aggregator**: Aggregator collects, pays label
3. **Rights Society**: Collects and distributes

### Payment Timeline
- Most platforms: Monthly statement, quarterly payment
- PRO: Quarterly or semi-annual
- Some platforms: Annual reporting

### Statement Review
- Royalty statement analysis
- Accuracy verification
- Discrepancy investigation
- Audit rights

## Common Issues

- Unclaimed royalties
- Incorrect split documentation
- Missing registration
- Platform payment delays
- Currency conversion confusion
