# Mechanicals

## Overview

Mechanical licenses authorize the reproduction of musical compositions, essential for distributing music on any format.

## Mechanical License Basics

### What It Covers
- Manufacturing of physical copies
- Digital downloads
- Interactive streaming
- Limited downloads
- Ringtones

### Who Pays
- Music distributors
- Record labels
- Digital platforms
- Artists (if self-publishing)

## License Types

### Statutory License
- Fixed rate set by government
- Available for most compositions
- No permission needed from publisher
- Current rate: $0.114 per track

### Direct License
- Negotiated with publisher
- Custom rates possible
- Higher or lower than statutory
- Greater flexibility

### Blanket License
- Covers multiple compositions
- Negotiated with rights societies
- Percentage-based
- Used by platforms

## Mechanical Collection

### Harry Fox Agency (HFA)
- Largest mechanical rights administrator
- Covers most U.S. publishers
- Collection and distribution
- Online licensing available

### Mechanical Licensing Collective (MLC)
- Non-profit organization
- Created by Music Modernization Act
- Blanket and bulk licensing
- Song ownership database

### Direct with Publishers
- Independent negotiation
- Custom terms
- Direct payment
- Ongoing relationship management

## Registration & Tracking

### Composition Registration
- Copyright registration
- HFA/MLC registration
- Publisher assignment
- Songwriter splits

### Licensing Process
1. Identify composition
2. Determine license type
3. Contact rights holder
4. Negotiate if needed
5. Execute license
6. Maintain records
7. Report usage and pay

## Payment Terms

### Statutory Rate
- Per-unit or percentage of revenue
- Quarterly or monthly reporting
- Payment within 30-60 days
- Detailed accounting required

### Audit Rights
- Licensor can audit
- Books and records access
- Annual reporting
- Discrepancy investigation

## Documentation

- License agreements
- Registration confirmation
- Payment records
- Usage reports
- Audit documentation
