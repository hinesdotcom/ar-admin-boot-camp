# FAQ

## General Questions

### What is A&R Administration?
A&R Administration manages the administrative, financial, and operational aspects of music releases, including budgets, contracts, clearances, and metadata coordination.

### Who should I contact for...?

**Budget Questions**: Finance Team
**Contract Issues**: Legal Department
**Recording Coordination**: Production Manager
**Sample Clearances**: Clearance Manager
**Release Planning**: A&R Manager

### Where can I find project information?
All project information is stored in UniPort. ATLAS tracks financial data. Contact your A&R manager for specific project access.

## Systems & Tools

### What is ATLAS?
ATLAS is our financial management system for budgets, vendor payments, and financial reporting.

### What is UniPort?
UniPort is the centralized database for release information, metadata, and rights tracking.

### How do I get access to these systems?
Contact IT Department with your department and role. Access is role-based and permission-dependent.

### What should I do if I can't log in?
Contact IT Help Desk with your username and the system name. Provide any error messages received.

## Financial Questions

### What is a Purchase Order (PO)?
A formal authorization for vendor services over $500. Required before work begins and must match the invoice.

### How long does payment take?
Typically 30-60 days from invoice receipt, depending on payment terms agreed with vendor.

### What's the difference between an advance and a royalty?
An advance is a payment made upfront against future royalties. Royalties are earned from sales/streams and used to recoup the advance.

## Recording & Production

### What formats should masters be delivered in?
WAV files, 24-bit/96kHz minimum. Specific requirements vary by use case.

### How much does recording typically cost?
Budgets vary greatly. Small indie projects: $5,000-$20,000. Major label projects: $50,000-$500,000+.

### What is the typical recording timeline?
Recording: 3-8 weeks. Mixing: 2-4 weeks. Mastering: 1-2 weeks. Total: 8-14 weeks typical.

## Contracts & Clearances

### Do I need sample clearances?
Yes. Any use of previously recorded material requires both master and publishing clearances.

### What is a talent release?
A talent release is an agreement where performers grant the label rights to use their performance on a recording.

### How do I get a contract reviewed?
Submit to Legal Department. Allow 1-2 weeks for review. Complex contracts may take longer.

## Publishing & Royalties

### What's the difference between mechanical and performance royalties?
**Mechanical**: Paid for reproduction (sales, downloads, streams)
**Performance**: Paid for public performance (radio, streaming, live venues)

### Do I need to register with a PRO?
Yes. Register your compositions with ASCAP, BMI, or SESAC to collect performance royalties.

### What is a mechanical license?
Permission to reproduce a composition, required for all formats. Statutory rate is $0.114 per track.

### How often do I get royalty statements?
Varies by source. Most platforms: monthly statements, quarterly payment. PROs: quarterly or semi-annual.

## Release & Distribution

### When should I submit releases to platforms?
Typically 4-6 weeks before release date for marketing coordination. Distribution must be 2-4 weeks minimum.

### What is the release readiness checklist?
All audio mastered, metadata complete, rights/clearances obtained, legal review done, financial reconciliation complete.

### How do I pitch to Spotify playlists?
Use Spotify for Artists Pitch Tool. Submit 2-4 weeks before release. Write compelling narrative and select appropriate mood tags.

### What's the cost to release on streaming platforms?
Distribution costs vary: Free-$50 per release typically through aggregators. Direct deals may have different terms.

## Troubleshooting

### My file was rejected by a platform. What do I do?
Check platform specifications (file format, metadata, artwork size). Common issues:
- Wrong file format
- Artwork under 3000x3000px
- Missing required metadata
- ISRC code conflicts

Contact platform support or your distribution partner for specific errors.

### There's a discrepancy in my royalty statement. What do I do?
1. Verify the calculation
2. Compare to previous statements
3. Check platform reporting
4. Contact the paying party
5. Request audit if necessary

### A vendor missed a deadline. What should I happen?
1. Notify vendor immediately
2. Clarify new timeline
3. Update project timeline
4. Notify affected teams
5. Document in project file
